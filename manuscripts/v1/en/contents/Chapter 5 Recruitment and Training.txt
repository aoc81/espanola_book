Chapter 5: Recruitment and Training of New Members
From digital recruitment to military transformation: the mechanisms of organizational reproduction

Española’s ability to maintain and expand its ranks, despite the inevitable casualties of prolonged combat, lies in a recruitment and training system that combines digital sophistication with competitive economic incentives and military training programs adapted to the realities of the modern battlefield. This system not only perpetuates the brigade’s organizational existence but also replicates and expands its distinctive culture, ensuring ideological continuity across generations of fighters.
The Digital Revolution in Military Recruitment
Española’s recruitment strategy represents a significant innovation in military recruitment, leveraging commercial social media platforms for purposes that traditionally required specialized state infrastructure. The main platforms—Telegram and VKontakte—were not arbitrarily chosen, but reflect a sophisticated understanding of the media habits of target populations.
Telegram, with its emphasis on privacy and broadcasting capabilities, provides an ideal channel for distributing content that could be problematic on more regulated platforms. The brigade’s official channels use this platform to share training videos, combat photographs, veterans’ testimonies, and—crucially—detailed information about compensation and benefits that function as powerful recruitment incentives.
VKontakte, as the dominant social media platform in Russia, allows for more specific targeting based on club affiliations, geographic location, and declared interests. This granularity in targeting is particularly valuable for a formation whose effectiveness depends on recruiting individuals with specific cultural backgrounds and appropriate ideological predispositions.
Recruitment materials distributed through these platforms exhibit professional production quality that rivals mainstream commercial campaigns. High-quality videos show fighters in training, awards ceremonies, and veterans’ testimonies, creating an aspirational narrative that presents service in the brigade as a form of personal fulfillment and social recognition.
The Economics of War: Competitive Financial Incentives
One of the most distinctive features of Española’s recruitment strategy is its transparency regarding financial compensation—an aspect many conventional military formations treat discreetly. The brigade’s promotional materials explicitly specify salary ranges, injury bonuses, and death payouts, presenting this information as selling points rather than administrative details.
The base salaries offered—between 220,000 and 344,000 rubles monthly (approximately €2,400–3,800)—represent significantly above-average compensation in Russia, especially for individuals with the educational levels and professional backgrounds typical of the target recruitment populations. For football hooligans, many of whom come from modest socioeconomic backgrounds, these salaries offer opportunities for economic mobility that would be difficult to achieve through conventional employment.
Injury bonuses, which can reach up to €131,000 depending on severity, function as war insurance that explicitly acknowledges the inherent risks of military service while providing tangible financial protection. This transparency about risks and compensation is in marked contrast to the more euphemistic approaches adopted by formal military institutions.
Death payouts—up to 5 million rubles (approximately €50,000) to relatives—represent substantial sums in the Russian economic context, particularly for working-class families. These payments can potentially transform death in service from a personal tragedy into a family economic opportunity, creating perverse incentives that may influence recruitment decisions.
Demographic Diversification: The Integration of Female Fighters
The decision to begin recruiting women in November 2023 represented a significant strategic expansion in the brigade’s recruitment base, challenging both football hooligan traditions and the norms of extremist formations. This evolution illustrates the primacy of operational considerations over cultural purity in Española’s organizational priorities.
The roles offered to female fighters include traditionally female-associated positions—medical services, communications—as well as more controversial roles such as snipers, drone operators, electronic warfare specialists, and even, exceptionally, assault units. This diversity in available opportunities suggests that female recruitment is not tokenism, but a genuine expansion of operational capabilities.
The integration of women has also required adaptations in messaging and promotional materials. Content directed at potential female recruits emphasizes themes such as personal empowerment, patriotic contribution, and professional opportunity, while maintaining consistency with the brigade’s broader organizational culture.
Compensation offered to female fighters is identical to that offered to their male counterparts, indicating that the brigade has adopted principles of equal pay that surpass many conventional military institutions. This economic parity likely functions as a significant incentive for women considering military service in contexts where gender pay gaps remain problematic.
Recruitment Profiles: Beyond Hooligans
While Española’s original core was primarily drawn from football hooligan cultures, the operational needs of modern conflict have forced expansion into more diverse profiles. Current recruitment campaigns actively seek individuals with specific technical skills that reflect the technological complexity of contemporary warfare.
Specialties in demand include:Reconnaissance Saboteurs: Individuals with experience in clandestine operations, infiltration, and intelligence gathering. These roles build on transferable skills from criminal backgrounds or previous military experience.Snipers: Requiring precision shooting skills that may derive from hunting experience, competitive shooting, or formal military training. The psychological profile necessary for sniping—patience, discipline, comfort with lethality—often overlaps with characteristics found in certain criminal or extremist backgrounds.Drone Operators: An entirely new specialty requiring technical aptitude more than physical prowess. This category has attracted individuals with engineering backgrounds, video gaming experience, or simply technological curiosity.Electronic Warfare Specialists: Requiring sophisticated technical knowledge to disrupt enemy communications and navigation systems. These positions often attract individuals with telecommunications backgrounds or advanced technical education.Air Defense Personnel: Operating portable anti-aircraft systems requires technical training but also quick decision-making under pressure. These roles often appeal to individuals with mechanical aptitude combined with aggressive personalities.
This diversification in specialties has necessitated corresponding adaptations in recruitment strategies, with targeted campaigns for different skill sets using vocabulary and incentives tailored for each demographic.
Training Centers: The Geography of Transformation
Española’s training infrastructure reflects both available resources and security considerations that shape its training programs. The main centers include facilities near Moscow and Saint Petersburg for initial processing and basic training, while more advanced and combat-specific training occurs in occupied territory in Ukraine.
The main training camp, located in the “pioneer camp ‘Zvezdochka’” in occupied territory, provides an environment where trainees can experience conditions similar to those encountered in actual combat. This proximity to active conflict zones allows for the integration of lessons learned from ongoing operations into training protocols, ensuring that preparation remains current with evolving battlefield conditions.
Training programs cover a comprehensive range of skills needed for modern warfare:Mine Clearance: A critical skill for operations in heavily contested areas, where explosive devices remain major hazards. Training includes identification of different mine types, safe disposal techniques, and route clearance procedures.Drone Operations: Both piloting commercially-available drones for reconnaissance and operating more sophisticated military-grade equipment for combat missions. Training includes maintenance, navigation, target identification, and attack procedures.Electronic Warfare: Understanding how to disrupt enemy communications, jam signals, and protect one’s own communications from similar attacks. These skills require both technical knowledge and tactical understanding of when and how to deploy different capabilities.Weapons Training: Utilizing both Russian-standard equipment and captured Ukrainian systems. Training emphasizes adaptability, as combatants may need to operate whatever equipment becomes available during operations.Urban Combat: Specifically designed for conflict environments characterized by dense civilian populations and complex terrain. Training includes building clearing, civilian interaction, and operating in environments where traditional military tactics may be inappropriate.
Screening Procedures: Selecting Appropriate Candidates
Despite the apparent openness of recruitment campaigns, Española maintains screening procedures designed to identify individuals likely to succeed within its organizational culture and operational requirements. These procedures, although not publicly documented, can be inferred from patterns in successful recruits and testimonials provided by brigade members.
Ideological compatibility appears to be a significant factor, though not necessarily requiring pre-existing extremist commitment. Instead, screening likely focuses on identifying individuals susceptible to radicalization within brigade culture rather than those already fully formed in their beliefs.
Physical fitness and psychological resilience are obviously important, but uniquely important for Española is the assessment of group loyalty and the ability to function within a tribal hierarchy. Individuals accustomed to independent action or uncomfortable with group conformity are likely to be filtered out during initial assessment.
Previous criminal history, rather than being disqualifying, often serves as confirmation of appropriate risk tolerance and willingness to operate outside conventional legal frameworks. However, crimes suggesting lack of discipline or reliability (such as drug addiction) are likely problematic.
Military experience is valued but not required, since brigade culture emphasizes loyalty and adaptability over technical expertise. Indeed, individuals with extensive formal military training may find brigade culture difficult to adapt to, as traditional military hierarchies differ significantly from the tribal loyalty structures that characterize hooligan cultures.
Retention and Career Development
Once individuals successfully complete initial training and begin combat operations, the brigade has developed systems designed to maintain morale and encourage career development within the organizational structure. These systems reflect the understanding that long-term effectiveness requires more than simply replacing casualties, but developing experienced cadres capable of training future generations.
Promotion opportunities within the brigade structure provide pathways for advancement that extend beyond simple combat effectiveness. Leadership roles in training programs, recruiting operations, and administrative functions offer alternatives for individuals whose combat effectiveness may decline due to age or injury but who retain value for the overall organization.
Recognition through decorations and media prominence provides non-monetary incentives that appeal to individuals whose motivation derives from status rather than purely financial considerations. The brigade’s extensive use of social media to highlight individual achievements creates opportunities for personal fame that can be attractive to certain personalities.
Integration with broader Russian military structures also provides pathways for career development that extend beyond the brigade itself. Successful brigade members can potentially transition to roles within conventional military units, private military companies, or security services, using experience gained within the brigade as credentials for further advancement.
Challenges and Limitations
Despite apparent success in maintaining recruitment levels, Española faces significant challenges in sustainable expansion that reflect broader issues with militarizing extremist subcultures. The pool of individuals possessing both appropriate cultural backgrounds and necessary skills is limited, forcing trade-offs between cultural authenticity and operational capability.
Geographic limitations also constrain recruitment effectiveness. While social media platforms enable nationwide outreach, the brigade’s effectiveness depends heavily on face-to-face relationships and cultural connections that are difficult to replicate purely through digital communication.
Competition with other military formations for similar recruitment pools creates pressure for increasingly attractive compensation packages that may become unsustainable if the conflict extends considerably. Additionally, casualty rates among experienced personnel require constant recruitment to replace expertise lost through combat deaths.
International sanctions and diplomatic pressure may eventually constrain access to the digital platforms currently used for recruitment, requiring the development of alternative communication methods that may be less effective for reaching target populations.
Future Directions
The evolution of recruitment and training practices within Española will likely reflect broader trends in both Russian military policy and international conflict dynamics. Increasing sophistication of weaponry and tactics requires corresponding evolution in training programs, while changing geopolitical circumstances may necessitate adaptations in targeting and messaging strategies.
The precedent established by Española in successfully militarizing extremist subcultures provides a template that may be replicated in other contexts, both within Russia and internationally. Understanding these mechanisms therefore becomes crucial for anticipating similar developments elsewhere.
