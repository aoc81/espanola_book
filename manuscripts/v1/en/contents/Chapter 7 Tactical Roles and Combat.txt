Chapter 7: Tactical Roles and Combat Specializations
The technical evolution of an irregular formation towards modern military capabilities

The transformation of Española from a collection of football hooligans into an effective military formation required not only organizational and cultural changes, but also the development of specialized technical capabilities reflecting the realities of the contemporary battlefield. This technical evolution represents one of the most fascinating aspects of Española experiment: the demonstration that irregular formations can develop levels of sophistication rivaling professional military units, provided they can access appropriate resources and maintain organizational flexibility.
Reconnaissance-Sabotage: The Historical Core
The reconnaissance-sabotage units of Española represent the direct continuation of the original “Skull and Bones” unit founded by Stanislav Orlov in 2014, providing organic continuity between the early Donbas conflict experiences and contemporary operations in Ukraine. This operational genealogy has allowed the brigade to maintain institutional expertise that many newer irregular formations lack.
Deep reconnaissance—the traditional specialty of these units—requires unique combinations of physical skills, technical capabilities, and psychological traits that align closely with the backgrounds of many brigade members. Experience in nocturnal urban navigation, developed over years of evading police surveillance during hooligan activities, translates directly into military infiltration capabilities.
Sabotage operations, however, have required significant technical adaptations that go beyond previous experience. The use of military explosives, electronic timing systems, and demolition techniques requires formal training, which the brigade has developed through a combination of expertise transferred from military veterans and structured training programs.
Española’s reconnaissance units have demonstrated particular effectiveness in operations requiring independent initiative and tactical adaptability. During the battles in Bakhmut and Avdiivka, these units provided crucial intelligence on enemy positions, supply routes, and defensive vulnerabilities, enabling successful operations by larger units.
However, these capabilities have also required significant investment in specialized equipment, going beyond what is traditionally available to irregular formations. Night vision equipment, encrypted communication systems, and satellite navigation devices have been necessary to maintain effectiveness in environments where enemy forces possess similar technological capabilities.
Snipers: Lethal Precision in Urban Combat
The development of sniper capabilities within Española illustrates both the possibilities and the limitations of specialized training in irregular formations. Unlike roles that can leverage prior experience, effective sniping requires combinations of technical skill, psychological discipline, and tactical knowledge that must be developed from the ground up.
The loss of Pavel “Joker” Apalkov, deputy commander of the sniper platoon, during the fighting in Chasiv Yar, highlighted both the importance of this specialization and the vulnerabilities created when critical expertise is concentrated in specific individuals. Apalkov represented not only immediate combat capability but also the institutional knowledge necessary for training future generations of specialists.
Española’s snipers have operated primarily in urban environments characterized by short ranges and targets of opportunity rather than traditional long-range precision shooting. This adaptation required the development of specific techniques for urban camouflage, rapid target acquisition, and coordinated operations with other unit types.
Weapons used by sniper units include both Russian-standard equipment and captured Ukrainian systems, requiring adaptability in maintenance and ammunition supply that adds logistical complexity. However, this diversity also provides operational flexibility when specific weapon systems become unavailable.
The integration of sniper operations with drone reconnaissance has represented a significant innovation, demonstrating the brigade’s capacity to combine traditional military techniques with emerging technologies. Drones provide targeting information that enables snipers to engage targets that would otherwise be difficult to locate and track.
Drone Operators: The New Technological Frontier
Española’s investment in training 100 drone operators, announced in February 2023, recognized that modern warfare increasingly depends on unmanned systems for reconnaissance, targeting, and direct-attack capabilities. This investment also illustrated the brigade’s willingness to allocate resources toward capabilities that require significant technical training but provide multiplicative effects on combat effectiveness.
Drone operations within the brigade encompass multiple specializations reflecting different tactical requirements:Reconnaissance Drones: Using commercially available systems modified for military purposes, providing real-time intelligence on enemy positions, movements, and activities. These systems have proved particularly valuable in urban environments where traditional reconnaissance methods are high-risk.Attack Drones: Modified commercial drones equipped with small explosives for precision targeting of specific individuals or equipment. While these systems lack the destructive power of military-grade weaponry, they provide cost-effective methods for engaging high-value targets.Electronic Warfare Drones: Specialized systems designed to disrupt enemy communications or navigation equipment. These systems require sophisticated technical knowledge but can provide significant tactical advantages when deployed effectively.Communications Relay Drones: Providing secure communications capabilities across distances or terrain that would otherwise require vulnerable ground-based relay stations.
The development of drone capabilities has required investment in both equipment and training infrastructure that goes beyond the resources of most irregular formations. Maintenance facilities, parts supply chains, and technical support systems have all required establishment or access through external networks.
The effectiveness of drone operations also depends heavily on integration with other combat capabilities. Coordination among drone operators, artillery units, and ground forces requires communication systems and protocols approaching those of conventional military formations.
Electronic Warfare: Dominating the Spectrum
The development of electronic warfare capabilities within Española represents one of the most significant innovations in terms of technical sophistication. Electronic warfare requires levels of technical expertise typically associated with specialized military or intelligence units rather than irregular formations.
The brigade’s electronic warfare capabilities include several specialized functions:Communications Jamming: Disrupting enemy radio communications through targeted interference that prevents coordination among opposing units. This capability is particularly valuable in urban combat where enemy units may depend heavily on radio coordination.GPS Disruption: Interfering with navigation systems used by enemy forces, which is especially important for countering precision-guided weapons and drone operations. This requires a sophisticated understanding of GPS signal structure and effective countermeasures.Signals Intelligence: Intercepting and analyzing enemy communications to gather tactical intelligence. This provides valuable information on enemy intentions, capabilities, and vulnerabilities.Electronic Counter-Countermeasures: Protecting the brigade’s own communications and navigation systems from similar attacks by enemy forces. This defensive capability is increasingly important as electronic warfare capabilities proliferate among both sides.
The development of these capabilities has required the recruitment of individuals with specialized technical backgrounds, representing a significant departure from the brigade’s traditional hooliganism-based recruitment. Engineers, telecommunications specialists, and individuals with advanced technical training have become increasingly important within the brigade’s structure.
Training programs for electronic warfare specialists require months of intensive instruction in both theoretical principles and practical applications. This investment of time and resources reflects the brigade’s recognition that technical capabilities are becoming ever more important to maintaining operational effectiveness.
Air Defense: Protection Against Aerial Threats
The inclusion of air defense capabilities within Española’s organizational structure reflects recognition that modern combat environments require protection against increasingly sophisticated aerial threats. Mikhail “Pitbull” Turkanov’s role as commander of the anti-aircraft battery illustrates both the importance of this specialization and the integration of controversial figures into technical roles.
The brigade’s air defense systems include primarily man-portable systems that can be operated by small teams, as opposed to large crew-served weapons that would require extensive logistical support. These systems provide protection against helicopters, low-flying aircraft, and some types of precision-guided munitions.
Training for air defense specialists encompasses multiple technical areas:Target Identification: Distinguishing between friendly and enemy aircraft, understanding aircraft capabilities and vulnerabilities, and predicting flight patterns for effective engagement.Systems Operation: Technical operation of specific weapon systems, including radar operation, missile guidance, and maintenance procedures.Tactical Integration: Coordinating air defense operations with other combat units to avoid fratricidal incidents while providing maximum protection for friendly forces.Mobility Operations: Rapid deployment and repositioning of air defense assets to avoid counter-battery fire while maintaining coverage over critical areas.
The effectiveness of air defense operations depends heavily on intelligence about enemy aviation capabilities and operational patterns. Integration with reconnaissance units and electronic warfare capabilities provides the information necessary for effective target engagement.
Anti-Tank Missiles: Anti-Armor Capabilities
The specialized anti-tank units within Española employ a combination of Russian-standard systems and captured Ukrainian equipment, requiring adaptability in training and operations that reflects the resource constraints faced by irregular formations.
Anti-tank capabilities encompass several weapon systems:Portable Anti-Tank Systems: Man-portable missiles that can be operated by small teams, providing the ability to engage armored vehicles while maintaining the mobility necessary for irregular operations.Heavy Anti-Tank Systems: Larger systems requiring vehicle mounting but offering greater range and destructive capability against heavily armored targets.Improvised Anti-Tank Devices: Field-expedient weapons created from available materials, requiring ingenuity and technical knowledge but providing cost-effective capabilities when standard systems are unavailable.
Training for anti-tank specialists must encompass both technical operation of weapons systems and tactical employment techniques. Target identification, range estimation, and engagement procedures require extensive practice to develop effectiveness under combat conditions.
Logistical requirements for anti-tank operations include not only the weapons systems themselves but also ammunition supply, maintenance, and transportation assets. These requirements often exceed resources readily available for irregular formations, necessitating external support networks.
Support Personnel: The Invisible Specialists
While combat specializations receive primary attention, the operational effectiveness of Española depends equally on specialized support personnel who provide critical services necessary for sustained operations.
Signalers: Communications specialists responsible for maintaining contact among dispersed units and coordinating with external support elements. Modern communications equipment requires technical expertise for effective operation and maintenance.
Driver-Mechanics: Vehicle operators and maintenance specialists responsible for transportation and equipment upkeep. Mechanical reliability is critical for the mobility-dependent operations typical of irregular formations.
Combat Medics: Medical personnel trained in battlefield medicine and casualty evacuation. Medical capabilities are essential for maintaining morale and minimizing combat losses that could compromise unit effectiveness.
Logistics Specialists: Personnel responsible for supply procurement, distribution, and inventory management. Logistical effectiveness directly impacts combat capability and operational sustainability.
These support specializations often require technical training equal to or greater than combat roles, but receive less attention in promotional materials that emphasize combat effectiveness. Nevertheless, degradation in support capabilities quickly compromises overall unit effectiveness regardless of combat proficiency.
Integration and Coordination Between Specialties
The true effectiveness of Española’s specialized capabilities depends on integration and coordination among different technical specializations rather than excellence in individual areas. Modern combat requires the simultaneous deployment of multiple capabilities that must work together to achieve tactical objectives.
Successful operations typically involve coordination among reconnaissance units that identify targets, electronic warfare specialists that disrupt enemy communications, drone operators that provide targeting information, and combat units that engage targets while receiving support from medical personnel and logistical assets.
This integration requires communication systems, standardized procedures, and training programs that enable different specializations to work together effectively. The development of these coordination capabilities represents one of the brigade’s most significant organizational achievements.
The future effectiveness of the brigade will depend heavily on maintaining and improving integration capabilities while continuing to develop individual specializations. Balanced investment across multiple technical areas, rather than concentration on single capabilities, appears necessary for sustained operational relevance.
