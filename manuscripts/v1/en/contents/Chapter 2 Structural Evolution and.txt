Chapter 2: Structural Evolution and Affiliations
The metamorphosis from a volunteer unit to the complex Russian state apparatus

The transformation of Española from its humble origins as a volunteer unit to its current status as an integral component of the Russian military apparatus perfectly illustrates the evolution of power structures in contemporary Russia. This organizational metamorphosis was not the result of strategic planning, but of pragmatic adaptation to the Kremlin’s changing needs and the opportunities presented by the Ukrainian conflict.
From Volunteers to Integrated Fighters
When Stanislav Orlov gathered his former “Skull and Bones” comrades in the spring of 2022, the group that would eventually become Española operated under the organizational umbrella of the Vostok Battalion, commanded by Alexander Khodakovsky in the self-proclaimed Donetsk People’s Republic. This initial affiliation provided local legitimacy and access to basic resources, but also imposed significant limitations in terms of operational autonomy.
The initial structure was relatively simple: veterans of the Donbas conflict served as the leadership core, while new recruits—mainly hooligans from various Russian football clubs—made up the main fighting force. However, this organizational simplicity soon proved inadequate for the formation’s growing ambitions.
During the battles of the Mariupol siege, particularly in operations against the Azovstal steel plant, the effectiveness demonstrated by Orlov’s unit attracted the attention of higher command levels. According to internal reports from the period, the brigade distinguished itself not only by its aggression in combat but also by its ability to maintain cohesion under extreme pressure, a trait directly attributable to the bonds forged in football hooligan culture.
In July 2022, this demonstrated effectiveness resulted in the integration of Española into the 114th Independent Motorized Rifle Brigade of Yenakievo-Danube, a promotion that reflected both military recognition and the need for more formal command structures for larger-scale operations.
Declaration of Independence: February 2023
The decisive moment in Española’s structural evolution came in February 2023, when the formation publicly announced its transition to private military company (PMC) status. This statement, broadcast through their official Telegram channels, claimed the organization had “550 people under its command, including 100 drone operators.”
The decision to adopt the PMC model was not merely administrative. As Orlov himself explained in later communications, this status allowed them to “independently decide where to fight” and exercise greater control over their internal operations. However, this apparent independence would prove to be more illusory than real.
The reorganization as a PMC also coincided with significant internal restructuring. In March 2023, Russian media reported that Española had formally split into two distinct wings: a military wing, responsible for battlefield operations, and a “humanitarian” wing responsible for publicity, recruitment, and fundraising.
This organizational bifurcation reflected a sophisticated understanding of modern operational requirements. The military wing could focus on combat capabilities, while the humanitarian wing developed the support functions necessary for the formation’s maintenance and expansion.
The Redut Umbrella: Controlled Autonomy
The true complexity of Española’s PMC status only became evident in the spring of 2023, when investigative reports revealed that the formation was operating under the control of Redut, an entity described as a “phantom company” linked to the Russian Ministry of Defense and the GRU.
Redut represents a paradigmatic example of the hybrid structures that characterize the contemporary Russian security apparatus. While ostensibly functioning as an independent PMC, Redut in practice serves as an intermediary between the Ministry of Defense and multiple irregular formations, managing administrative, logistical, and financial functions that allow the Russian state to maintain a certain distance from these operations.
For Española, subordination to Redut provided significant advantages. Fighters could sign formal contracts with a civilian entity, receive regular payments from the Ministry of Defense, and access military-grade equipment and supplies, all while maintaining the appearance of operational independence.
This structure also allowed for important financial flexibility. Through Redut, Española could receive funds from multiple sources—including the state budget, contributions from private oligarchs, and donations collected by their humanitarian wing—without the bureaucratic restrictions that characterized conventional military units.
Integration into the Volunteer Corps: 2024
The structural evolution of Española experienced another significant turning point in the second half of 2024, when the formation was integrated into the “Volunteer Corps” structures of the Ministry of Defense of the Russian Federation, formally adopting the designation of 88th Reconnaissance and Assault Brigade.
This integration represented the official recognition of the formation’s effectiveness, but also marked the end of its relative operational autonomy. As part of the Volunteer Corps, Española became subject to conventional military chains of command and standardized bureaucratic procedures.
However, integration did not entirely erase the brigade’s distinctive characteristics. Its unique composition, specialized recruitment methods, and organizational culture developed during its years as an irregular formation continued to set it apart from conventional military units.
Internal Organization: The Two Wings
The organizational structure developed by Española during its period as an independent PMC provided a model for the division of labor between military and support functions that has since been adopted by other Russian irregular formations.
The Military Wing
The military wing of Española is organized into specialized units that reflect both lessons learned in combat and the specific capabilities of its personnel. These include:
●	Reconnaissance-Sabotage Units: The historical core of the formation, direct heirs of the original “Skull and Bones” unit
●	Sniper Platoons: Specialized in long-distance precision operations
●	Drone Operators: A capability developed specifically for the Ukrainian conflict
●	Electronic Warfare Specialists: Focused on countering Ukrainian communication and navigation capabilities
●	Air Defense Units: Equipped with portable anti-aircraft systems
●	Anti-Tank Missile Teams: Using both Russian systems and captured equipment
●	Support Personnel: Including signalers, driver-mechanics, and combat medics
This specialization reflects a sophisticated understanding of the requirements of the modern battlefield, where effectiveness depends on the integration of multiple technical capabilities.
The Humanitarian Wing
The humanitarian wing, led by figures such as Ilya Khanin and Alexey Trifonov, acts as the public interface of the brigade and its engine for organizational growth. Its functions include:
●	Recruitment: Managing personnel recruitment campaigns through social networks and public events
●	Fundraising: Coordinating private donations and corporate sponsorships
●	Public Relations: Maintaining the brigade’s media presence and managing its public narrative
●	Supply: Providing equipment, food, and other resources necessary for military operations
●	Community Programs: Developing initiatives in occupied territories, such as the children’s football team established in Horlivka
The sophistication of these support operations distinguishes Española from more rudimentary irregular formations, providing capabilities that rival those of professional military organizations.
Networks of Multiple Loyalties
One of the most intriguing features of Española’s organizational structure is its capacity to maintain multiple simultaneous loyalties without apparent internal conflict. The formation operates simultaneously as:
●	An extension of the Russian Ministry of Defense through its integration into the Volunteer Corps
●	A client of the GRU’s services through its relationship with Redut
●	An instrument of the Rotenberg oligarchs through funding provided by Viktor Shendrik
●	An independent organization with its own cultural and political agenda
This multiplicity of affiliations could, in theory, generate significant conflicts of interest. However, available evidence suggests that these different loyalties have been maintained in dynamic balance, with each relationship providing specific resources and opportunities.
The Spanish Model: Replication and Adaptation
The structural evolution of Española has provided an organizational template that has influenced the development of other Russian irregular formations. Elements such as the division between military and humanitarian wings, the use of PMC structures as funding vehicles, and the maintenance of distinctive cultural identities within formal organizational frameworks, have been adopted by subsequent units.
This influence extends beyond the Russian context. International military observers have identified elements of the “Spanish model” in emerging irregular formations in other conflict theaters, suggesting that the organizational innovations developed by Orlov and his team have contributed to the broader evolution of contemporary irregular warfare.
Contradictions and Structural Tensions
Despite its apparent organizational success, the structure of Española contains inherent tensions that could affect its long-term stability. The most significant of these is the tension between cultural autonomy and state control. While the formation’s effectiveness partly depends on its distinctive identity and unique organizational culture, its progressive integration into formal state structures threatens to dilute precisely those characteristics that make it effective.
Additionally, the multiplicity of funding sources, while providing operational flexibility, also creates potential vulnerabilities. Changes in the priorities of individual sponsors, tensions between different power centers, or state budgetary pressures could destabilize the delicate balance that keeps the formation operational.
Future Prospects
The evolutionary trajectory of Española from volunteer unit to integral component of the Russian military apparatus suggests broader trends in the organization of military force in contemporary authoritarian states. The ability to integrate distinctive irregular formations within formal state structures, while maintaining their unique operational characteristics, represents a significant organizational innovation with implications that extend beyond the specific Russian context.
In the next chapter, we will examine how this sophisticated organizational structure both facilitates and is facilitated by the unique composition of Española: the transformation of football hooligans and ultras into effective fighters, and the recruitment strategies that make this process possible.
