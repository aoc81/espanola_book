Chapter 11: Public Image Management and Wartime Narrative
Between digital propaganda and criticism of high command: the media construction of a controversial military identity

Española has developed one of the most sophisticated and paradoxical communication strategies observed in contemporary irregular military formations. Its approach to public image management combines exceptional transparency with carefully constructed narratives, using modern digital platforms to project both military professionalism and subcultural authenticity. This communication strategy not only facilitates recruitment and fundraising, but also allows the brigade to maintain an independent voice that at times challenges official Russian narratives.
The Digital Ecosystem: Telegram and VKontakte as Pillars
Española’s media strategy centers primarily on two digital platforms that reflect both the technical realities of the Russian environment and the specific communication needs of irregular military organizations. Telegram, with its focus on privacy and mass broadcast capabilities, serves as the main channel for official communications, while VKontakte provides access to broader Russian audiences and facilitates targeted demographic outreach.
The brigade’s official Telegram channels operate as comprehensive multimedia hubs, combining operational updates, recruitment materials, propaganda content, and political commentary. The production quality of this content rivals that of professional media, indicating significant investment in communication capabilities that go far beyond the amateur efforts typical of irregular formations.
The use of Telegram also provides operational security benefits crucial for organizations operating in conflict environments. The platform’s encryption capabilities allow communications to evade interception by adversary intelligence services, while its mass broadcast functions eliminate the need to maintain contact databases that could compromise security if captured.
VKontakte, as Russia’s dominant social media platform, enables more granular targeting based on geographic location, sports affiliations, and demographic data that facilitate precise recruitment campaigns. This platform also allows for bidirectional interactions that Telegram limits, facilitating direct engagement with potential recruits and supporters.
Teddy Boy Greg: The Unlikely Propagandist
One of the most intriguing figures in Española’s media apparatus is Greg, known as “Teddy Boy Greg,” an American expatriate who serves as the brigade’s English-language media representative. His presence illustrates both the international ambition of the formation’s communication strategy and the ideological contradictions that mark its public narrative.
Greg, described as a barber with the nickname “the racist rockabilly,” moved to Russia in 2017 and has developed a distinctive media presence that combines American subcultural aesthetics with extremist pro-Russian messaging. His role as a cultural intermediary enables the brigade to communicate with English-speaking audiences in ways that would be difficult for native Russian speakers.
Greg’s controversial statements, particularly his documented racist commentary on various platforms, serve multiple strategic purposes for the brigade. First, they establish ideological credentials that resonate with international extremist audiences. Second, they generate media attention that amplifies the brigade’s messaging reach well beyond circles traditionally interested in Russian affairs. Third, they provide plausible deniability for extremist positions expressed through a figure who can be characterized as expressing personal views rather than official brigade policy.
However, the prominence of figures like Greg also creates vulnerabilities for the brigade. His American background and controversial statements provide material that opponents can use to characterize the brigade as reliant on foreign extremists, potentially undermining narratives about authentic Russian patriotism.
The Paradox of Military Transparency
Española’s communication strategy presents a fundamental paradox: a military formation operating in active combat conditions maintains a media presence that provides detailed information about its operations, personnel, and activities. This transparency stands in stark contrast to the operational security practices typical of professional military units.
The decision to maintain high media visibility reflects strategic calculations that prioritize recruitment and fundraising benefits over traditional security concerns. The brigade has apparently concluded that the advantages derived from public engagement outweigh the risks associated with providing information that could be useful to opposing forces.
This transparency also serves legitimacy purposes that help distinguish the brigade from formations that operate entirely clandestinely. Public visibility creates accountability mechanisms that can reassure potential supporters about the brigade’s activities, while also providing evidence of effectiveness that supports fundraising efforts.
However, this approach also creates vulnerabilities that extend beyond immediate operational concerns. Detailed public information about brigade activities facilitates intelligence collection by opposing services and provides evidence that could be used in future legal proceedings against brigade members.
Criticism of Russian High Command: An Independent Voice
One of the most notable characteristics of Española’s public communication has been its willingness to criticize aspects of the official conduct of the war by the Russian Ministry of Defense. The formation’s Telegram account has expressed criticisms of the invasion’s management, notably stating that while the Wagner Group’s decision to mutiny was catastrophic, its harsh critiques of key figures like Sergei Shoigu and Valery Gerasimov were valid.
This position represents a sophisticated communicative balance that maintains loyalty to the overall Russian geopolitical project while expressing frustrations with tactical and strategic decisions by the military leadership. By endorsing the substance of Wagner’s criticisms while condemning their methods, the brigade positions itself as a responsible voice of constructive criticism rather than seditious opposition.
The willingness to criticize senior military leadership also serves internal morale purposes. Combat personnel often develop frustrations with command decisions that appear divorced from battlefield realities, and providing an outlet for these concerns can help maintain unit cohesion that might otherwise be undermined by suppressed grievances.
However, public criticism of military leadership also creates potential conflicts with the brigade’s dependence on state support and coordination. Balancing independence of voice with maintenance of essential relationships requires careful navigation that could become complicated if criticisms are perceived as exceeding acceptable boundaries.
Mainstream Media Coverage: Normalization
Española has achieved notable levels of coverage in Russian mainstream media that help normalize its presence within broader societal discourse. This coverage extends beyond specialized military publications to include mainstream news outlets, sports media, and entertainment platforms that reach audiences far beyond traditional military constituencies.
In late 2022, it was reported that a host from Channel One covering the brigade posted photos with the organization’s neo-Nazi symbols on her Telegram channel. This incident illustrates both the level of mainstream media attention the brigade receives and the complications that arise when mainstream media figures become associated with controversial symbols.
The willingness of mainstream media figures to publicly associate with the brigade suggests that its activities have achieved levels of social acceptability that extend beyond fringe extremist communities. This mainstreaming provides legitimacy that facilitates broader social support and resource mobilization.
However, mainstream associations also create vulnerabilities when symbols or activities associated with the brigade generate controversy that could affect the careers of media figures. Potential backlash against mainstream supporters could reduce the brigade’s access to mainstream platforms that have been important for its communications strategy.
Sporting Events: Propaganda in Action
The integration of the brigade’s messaging into Russian sporting events represents one of the most effective innovations in its communication strategy. In September 2023, CSKA Moscow hockey teams displayed Spanish Brigade symbols on their uniforms during games, providing exposure to audiences that likely would not encounter brigade messaging through traditional military channels.
Sports events provide particularly valuable propaganda opportunities because they attract audiences seeking entertainment rather than political content, potentially reducing psychological resistance to messaging that might be rejected in more obviously political contexts. Association with successful sports teams also provides positive associations that can enhance the brigade's image.
The choice of hockey, rather than football, for these displays also reflects strategic communication thinking. Hockey enjoys higher social status in Russia and attracts more affluent audiences who may have greater capacity for financial support. Hockey associations also avoid some of the negative stereotypes associated with football hooliganism that could complicate mainstream acceptance.
However, sports-based propaganda also creates potential complications if athletes or teams face criticism for displaying controversial symbols. International sports federations increasingly monitor political displays that could result in sanctions against participating athletes or organizations.
Celebrity Visits: Legitimization through Association
The brigade has cultivated relationships with prominent figures in Russian sports and entertainment that provide legitimacy through public association. In December 2023, football coaches Kurban Berdyev and Evgeny Kaleshin visited Spanish Brigade fighters in Mariupol, providing photo opportunities that reinforce narratives about civilian support for brigade activities.
These visits serve multiple purposes beyond simple public relations. They provide morale benefits for combat personnel who may feel isolated from civilian society, while also creating obligations for prominent visitors who become personally invested in the success of individuals they have met. Personal relationships generated during these visits may translate into ongoing support that extends beyond single publicity events.
The willingness of prominent sports figures to visit combat zones also demonstrates commitment levels that help validate the brigade’s importance within broader Russian society. Public figures typically avoid unnecessary risks for purely ceremonial purposes, so their presence suggests that they view the brigade as having significant importance.
However, associations with public figures also create potential vulnerabilities when those individuals face controversies that could affect the brigade’s reputation. Public figures often face scrutiny that could extend to their associations, potentially creating complications for organizations that depend on their support.
Content Production: Professional Quality
The quality of multimedia content produced by the brigade’s communications team rivals that of professional media organizations, indicating significant investment in production capabilities that go beyond amateur efforts typical of irregular formations. Video content includes professional editing, high-quality graphics, and sophisticated narratives that demonstrate an understanding of modern media consumption patterns.
Production quality enables the brigade to compete effectively in media environments saturated with professional content. Amateur-quality communications often fail to attract attention in competitive media landscapes, while professional production values help ensure that messaging reaches intended audiences.
High production values also contribute to perceptions of legitimacy that help distinguish the brigade from formations that may appear less organized or professional. Visual presentation affects audience perceptions about organizational competence, which can influence decisions about support or participation.
However, professional-quality communications also require significant resource investments that must be sustained over time. Production capabilities require equipment, technical expertise, and ongoing maintenance that represent opportunity costs that could alternatively be devoted to direct military capabilities.
Future Communications Challenges
The brigade’s communications strategy faces several potential challenges that may require strategic adaptations. International sanction regimes increasingly target digital platforms and communication tools that could restrict access to current channels. Platform restrictions could force migration to alternative systems that may be less effective for reaching intended audiences.
Domestic political changes could affect tolerances for public criticism of military leadership that currently characterize the brigade’s communications. Changes in acceptable discourse boundaries could require modifications in messaging strategies that balance independence with compliance.
Nonetheless, the demonstrated success in developing sophisticated communications capabilities suggests that the brigade possesses organizational learning capacity that could enable adaptation to changing circumstances. Understanding the effective communications techniques developed by the brigade provides insights that may be applicable in other contexts where irregular formations seek to develop public support.
