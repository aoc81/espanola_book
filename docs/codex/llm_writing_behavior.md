# Writing Protocol — From Hooligans to War Machines

Behavioral guidelines to reduce common LLM and analytical mistakes when writing this book.  
Merge with project-specific methodology and sourcing rules.

**Tradeoff:** These guidelines prioritize rigor, traceability, and narrative integrity over speed or stylistic flourish.

---

## 1. Think Before Writing

Do not assume coherence. Do not smooth contradictions. Surface uncertainty.

Before drafting:

- State assumptions explicitly (timeline, actors, affiliations).
- If multiple interpretations exist, present them — do not silently choose one.
- Distinguish clearly between:
  - Verified facts
  - Claims by actors (e.g., Española itself)
  - External allegations or intelligence assessments
- If a claim is weakly sourced or contested, say so.
- If something is unclear or contradictory, stop and flag it.

**Rule:** Ambiguity is not a problem to fix — it is often the story.

---

## 2. Evidence First, Narrative Second

The narrative must emerge from the evidence — not the reverse.

- Every materially relevant claim must be traceable to a source.
- Attribute explicitly:
  - Media investigations
  - Telegram statements
  - Government or intelligence-linked assessments
- Never present self-descriptions as fact:
  - Example: Española calling itself a “volunteer brotherhood” ≠ objective reality :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}
- When sources diverge:
  - Show the divergence
  - Avoid forced synthesis

**Test:** Could a reader reconstruct the evidence chain from the text?

---

## 3. Simplicity Over Exhaustiveness

Do not confuse density with clarity.

- No unnecessary subplots or speculative links.
- No over-explaining basic geopolitical context.
- Avoid listing facts without narrative function.
- Each paragraph must answer: *why does this matter here?*

If a section feels like a data dump, rewrite it.

**Guiding question:** Would an editor cut this paragraph without losing meaning?

---

## 4. Surgical Writing

Touch only what serves the argument.

When revising:

- Do not rewrite entire sections for stylistic reasons.
- Do not “improve” tone if it dilutes precision.
- Do not introduce new claims during editing.
- Keep alignment with existing narrative voice.

If you notice:
- Gaps → flag them
- Weak claims → qualify them
- Redundant passages → compress them

**Rule:** Every sentence must earn its place.

## 6. Source-Aware Writing

Not all sources are equal. Treat them accordingly.

### Source categories:

- **Internal / propaganda (Telegram, VK)**
  - High value for intent, identity, messaging
  - Low reliability for factual claims

- **Independent investigations (Meduza, Jamestown, etc.)**
  - Analytical but potentially biased
  - Require cross-checking

- **Academic / analytical papers**
  - Strong for context and patterns
  - Often weak on real-time specificity

- **Adversarial or intelligence-linked sources**
  - May contain accurate insights
  - Must be framed carefully, not adopted wholesale

**Rule:** Always write *from the source, not as the source.*

---

## 7. Character and Structure Discipline

Avoid flattening actors into archetypes.

- Do not reduce Española to:
  - “neo-Nazi unit”
  - “patriotic volunteers”
  - “state proxy”
- It is all of these, depending on lens and moment.

When introducing figures:

- Anchor them in:
  - Biography
  - Role in the network
  - Symbolic function (propaganda, recruitment, command)

Example:
- A commander may be simultaneously:
  - A former hooligan
  - A battlefield leader
  - A propaganda figure 

---

## 8. Goal-Driven Chapters

Each chapter must have a clear analytical function.

Transform vague goals into concrete outcomes:

- “Explain Española” →  
  “Show how a hooligan subculture became a semi-institutionalized combat unit”

- “Describe structure” →  
  “Demonstrate how decentralized recruitment coexists with centralized control”

- “Analyze funding” →  
  “Map the hybrid model: grassroots donations + elite patronage”

Each chapter should:

1. Pose a question  
2. Develop evidence  
3. Reach a grounded conclusion (even if partial)

---

## 9. Avoid Narrative Temptations

Resist what *sounds good* but weakens the work:

- Over-dramatization of violence
- Clean origin stories
- Linear evolution (reality is messy)
- Moral simplification

Example:
The transformation from hooligans to soldiers is not a “conversion” —  
it is a gradual convergence of violence, identity, and opportunity.

---

## 10. Maintain Investigative Voice

Target tone:

- Controlled, not sensational
- Analytical, but human
- Narrative, but evidence-driven

Avoid:

- Academic dryness
- Journalistic superficiality
- Fiction-like dramatization

Aim for:

> A reader who feels guided through complexity, not convinced by rhetoric.

---

## Final Test

Before validating any section, ask:

- Can every key claim be traced to a source?
- Are contradictions visible or artificially resolved?
- Is anything written because it “sounds right” rather than being evidenced?
- Would a critical reader trust this passage?

If any answer is “no” → rewrite.
