Chapter 9: Sources of Funds and Fundraising Mechanisms
The humanitarian wing as an engine of growth and social legitimization

The long-term sustainability of any irregular military formation depends not only on elite sponsorships and state support, but also on its ability to generate diversified funding streams that provide both material resources and social legitimacy. Española has developed one of the most sophisticated civil-military fundraising systems observed in contemporary irregular formations, using a combination of “humanitarian” initiatives, sports events, and community activities that simultaneously generate income and reinforce its narrative as legitimate defenders of Russian patriotic values.
Organizational Division: Military vs. Humanitarian
In March 2023, Russian media reported that Española had formally split into two distinct wings: a military wing in charge of battlefield operations, and a “humanitarian” wing responsible for publicity, recruitment, and fundraising. This organizational bifurcation was not merely administrative, but reflected a strategic understanding that modern military effectiveness requires civil support capabilities that go far beyond the traditional functions of irregular formations.
The humanitarian wing essentially operates as a hybrid non-profit organization combining genuinely charitable activities with objectives of propaganda and military fundraising. This structure allows the brigade to present a respectable public face that can interact with civilian institutions, corporations, and ordinary citizens without the negative connotations associated with purely military organizations.
The legitimacy of this division is reinforced through formal registration with Russian authorities and compliance with applicable regulations for charitable organizations. This legality provides protection against government investigations while facilitating access to funding channels that would be unavailable to purely military entities.
However, the division between military and humanitarian wings also creates potential vulnerabilities related to coordination and resource allocation. Different priorities between these wings can generate internal tensions that affect overall organizational effectiveness.
Ilya Khanin: Political Connections and Logistics Management
Ilya Khanin, identified as a key member of Española’s humanitarian wing and brother of the chief of the Ramensky District in Moscow, exemplifies the kind of local political connections that facilitate effective fundraising operations. His dual role as logistical coordinator for the battalion and community outreach specialist illustrates how family political ties can be leveraged for military support activities.
Khanin’s brother’s position as district chief in Moscow provides access to local government networks that can facilitate various support activities. Local government connections can assist with permits for events, access to public venues, coordination with law enforcement for public activities, and referrals to potential corporate sponsors within the jurisdiction.
Khanin’s responsibilities encompass both practical logistics—ensuring that combat units receive necessary supplies and equipment—and public outreach activities designed to maintain civilian support and generate donations. This combination requires skill sets that include both administrative competence and public relations capabilities.
However, family political connections also create potential complications if political winds shift or if Khanin’s brother faces political difficulties. Dependencies on personal relationships rather than institutional systems create vulnerabilities that may affect organizational stability.
Alexey Trifonov: The Humanitarian Veteran
Alexey Trifonov represents continuity between early support activities for Russian forces in Donbas and current humanitarian operations. His involvement with the group dating back to 2014, when he provided humanitarian aid to fighters, demonstrates long-term commitment that extends beyond opportunistic participation in high-profile military operations.
Trifonov’s experience since 2014 provides institutional memory about effective methods for civilian-military coordination, donor relations, and logistical support that would be difficult for newer participants to replicate. This historical perspective informs current strategies and helps avoid mistakes that might otherwise compromise effectiveness.
Long-term participants like Trifonov also serve crucial roles in maintaining organizational culture and values during periods of rapid expansion or change. Their presence provides continuity that helps integrate new members while preserving distinctive characteristics that define organizational identity.
However, reliance on long-term participants also creates succession planning challenges if these individuals become unavailable due to injury, political problems, or other circumstances that could affect their continued participation.
Community Initiatives: Beyond Fundraising
Española’s humanitarian wing has developed several community initiatives that serve multiple purposes including fundraising, public relations, and creating sustainable civilian support networks. One of the most visible initiatives has been the establishment of a children’s football team in Horlivka, demonstrating commitment to long-term community development in occupied territories.
The children’s football team serves several strategic purposes beyond simple community service. First, it provides visible evidence of constructive contributions that counter narratives about purely destructive military activities. Second, it creates positive associations with the brigade among families and community members that may translate into future support. Third, it establishes infrastructure for youth engagement that could eventually contribute to recruitment pipelines.
Community sports initiatives also provide opportunities for identifying and cultivating future supporters who may contribute financial or logistical support. Parents and community members involved in children’s activities often develop personal relationships that can be leveraged for various types of assistance.
However, community initiatives in occupied territories also create potential security vulnerabilities if opposing forces target civilian infrastructure associated with the brigade. Attacks on community facilities could generate negative publicity that undermines broader support activities.
Sports Support: Extension into Hockey
While the brigade’s origins in football hooliganism provided natural connections to football-related support networks, expansion into other sports has demonstrated broader appeal within Russian athletic communities. In September 2023, CSKA Moscow hockey teams displayed Spanish Brigade symbols on their uniforms during games, indicating institutional support that extends beyond individual athlete preferences.
Hockey support is particularly significant because hockey enjoys higher social status in Russia than football hooliganism, associating the brigade with more mainstream and respectable athletic communities. Hockey players and fans typically have higher socioeconomic status that translates to greater financial capacity for supporting charitable causes.
Institutional support from hockey teams also provides access to corporate sponsorship networks that are typically more sophisticated than those available through football hooligan communities. Hockey sponsors often include major corporations that have greater financial capacity and may be interested in broader patriotic causes.
However, high-profile sports endorsements also create greater visibility that may attract unwanted attention from opposition groups or international sanctions regimes targeting Russian sports organizations.
Specialized Recruitment Events
Beyond traditional outreach activities, the humanitarian wing has organized specialized recruitment events targeted at specific professional communities that possess skills needed for modern military operations. In July 2023, Española held a recruitment event in Moscow for engineers and telecommunications specialists, demonstrating sophisticated understanding of manpower needs and targeted outreach strategies.
Professional recruitment events serve the dual purpose of generating both human resources and financial support. Attendees who may not be suitable for or interested in direct military participation may nonetheless become financial supporters or provide professional services that support brigade operations.
Targeting specific professional communities also allows for more effective resource allocation compared to generic outreach efforts. Events can be tailored to address specific concerns and interests of target audiences, increasing effectiveness while reducing costs associated with broad-spectrum marketing.
However, professional recruitment events also increase visibility among educated populations who may be more skeptical of brigade activities and more likely to report concerns to authorities if they observe problematic behaviors.
Donation Mechanisms and Financial Transparency
The humanitarian wing has developed sophisticated mechanisms for accepting donations that balance accessibility for potential donors with security requirements that protect both donors and the brigade from potential legal complications. These mechanisms include both digital payment systems that allow anonymous contributions and traditional methods that enable face-to-face transactions.
Digital donation systems utilize encrypted payment processors that protect donor identity while ensuring funds reach intended recipients. These systems allow supporters to contribute without exposing themselves to potential retaliation or unwanted attention from authorities or opposition groups.
Traditional donation methods, including cash collections at events and direct transfers through intermediary organizations, provide alternatives for supporters who prefer not to leave digital traces or who lack access to electronic payment systems.
Financial transparency practices include periodic reporting on the use of donated funds that demonstrate responsible stewardship while maintaining operational security. Donors receive updates on activities supported by their contributions without compromising sensitive operational information.
Fundraising for Specific Equipment
One of the most effective strategies developed by the humanitarian wing involves targeted fundraising campaigns for specific equipment needs that can be clearly articulated to potential donors. Rather than requesting general financial support, these campaigns focus on specific items that serve obvious military purposes while appealing to civilian sensibilities.
Medical equipment campaigns emphasize the humanitarian aspects of providing battlefield medical care, appealing to donors who may be uncomfortable with directly funding weapons but support saving lives. These campaigns can generate substantial funds while maintaining plausible civilian purposes.
Communications equipment campaigns emphasize safety and coordination needs that resonate with civilian understanding of emergency services requirements. Donors can feel confident that their contributions serve protective rather than purely aggressive purposes.
Vehicle and logistics campaigns appeal to a practical understanding of transportation needs that translate easily to civilian contexts. Supporters can relate to these needs based on their own experiences with transportation challenges.
Partnerships with Charitable Organizations
The humanitarian wing has developed partnerships with established charitable organizations that provide both legitimacy and operational capabilities that would be difficult to develop independently. In May 2025, MEMRI reported that the neo-Nazi Spanish Brigade was raising funds for drones and equipment via a charitable foundation linked to the Russian party “Green Alternative.”
Partnerships with established charities provide access to donor networks, administrative capabilities, and legal frameworks that enable more sophisticated fundraising operations. Established organizations possess expertise in donor relations, financial management, and regulatory compliance that would require significant investment to develop internally.
However, charitable partnerships also create potential reputation risks if partner organizations become controversial or if opposition groups publicize connections between established charities and military organizations that may have problematic characteristics.
Sustainable Funding Models
The long-term sustainability of the brigade’s civilian funding efforts depends on developing diversified revenue streams that can withstand changes in political climate, economic conditions, and public opinion. Current efforts encompass multiple approaches that reduce dependence on any single funding source.
Regular donor programs provide predictable monthly revenue that enables long-term planning while reducing dependence on sporadic large donations that may not be sustainable. These programs create ongoing relationships that may be more resilient to temporary controversies or political changes.
Corporate sponsorship relationships provide larger funding amounts but require ongoing maintenance and may be more sensitive to political changes that affect corporate willingness to associate with controversial organizations.
Event-based fundraising provides opportunities for large single donations but requires significant organizational investment and may not be repeatable if circumstances change.
Future Challenges and Opportunities
The continued effectiveness of the brigade’s civilian fundraising efforts faces several challenges that may require strategic adaptations. International sanctions increasingly target Russian organizations that support military activities, potentially restricting access to certain types of donors or payment systems.
Domestic political changes could affect the legal environment for charitable activities associated with military organizations, requiring adaptations in operational methods or legal structures.
However, sustained conflict also creates ongoing demands for military support that may generate continued civilian willingness to contribute resources, particularly if brigade operations are perceived as successful and aligned with broader Russian strategic objectives.
The precedent established by Española in developing sophisticated civilian-military fundraising capabilities provides a model that may be replicated by other organizations, both within Russia and internationally. Understanding these mechanisms therefore becomes crucial for anticipating similar developments elsewhere.
