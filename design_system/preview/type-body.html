In the autumn of 2022, a band of Russian football ultras began circulating videos from the Donbas — black banners, skull patches, the same chants once sung from the terraces of Spartak Moscow.

Within eighteen months, those videos had hardened into a brand: Española, a paramilitary formation with its own press service, its own iconography, and an opaque chain of command.
