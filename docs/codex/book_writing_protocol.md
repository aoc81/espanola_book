# Book Writing Protocol — Española Project

## Purpose

A standing project guide for drafting all chapters of the co-authored investigative book on Española.

## Core voice

Write in a human, polished editor-in-chief voice suitable for long-form investigative nonfiction.

The prose should be:

* narratively strong but controlled
* vivid without melodrama
* analytically sharp without sounding academic
* elegant in transitions and structure
* unmistakably human rather than synthetic or report-like

## Narrative method

Each chapter should do more than recount facts. It should:

1. open with a scene, contradiction, or revealing moment when appropriate
2. connect individuals to systems, networks, and political logic
3. widen naturally from episode to structure
4. maintain momentum while preserving factual restraint

## Evidentiary rules

These are mandatory.

### 1) Source every relevant episode

Every materially relevant episode must be tied to source support.

This includes, when applicable:

* leadership changes
* deployments and battlefield episodes
* recruitment and propaganda actions
* financial or patronage links
* sanctions or legal designations
* organizational restructurings
* public statements
* reported deaths, detentions, or disbandment

### 2) Distinguish claim types

Use the correct wording depending on the strength and nature of the evidence.

* **Documented fact:** state directly and cite.
* **Attribution-dependent claim:** use formulations such as “according to…”, “as reported by…”, “open-source material indicates…”.
* **Contested or uncertain claim:** make the uncertainty explicit in the prose.
* **Self-description or promotional claim by Española or affiliated channels:** never present as unqualified fact unless independently corroborated.

### 3) Preserve narrative readability

The narrative should remain fluid. Citation support should sit underneath the prose, not suffocate it.

Preferred model:

* strong narrative in the main text
* disciplined notes/endnotes/footnotes attached to relevant episodes

## Style guardrails

Avoid:

* dry academic exposition
* unsupported grand claims
* sensationalism
* excessive adjective stacking
* copy that sounds like advocacy, prosecution brief, or intelligence memo unless the section specifically requires it

Prefer:

* precise verbs
* scene-based openings when earned
* analytical paragraphs that interpret rather than merely list
* calm authority
* explicit handling of ambiguity where evidence is mixed

## Structural principle

The book should work on two levels at once:

* **Narrative layer:** readable, human, compelling
* **Evidentiary layer:** rigorous, inspectable, source-driven

## Default chapter checklist

Before considering a chapter draft complete, verify:

* the opening has narrative pull
* the chapter advances both story and analysis
* each relevant episode has source support
* contested claims are attributed correctly
* unit self-descriptions are labeled as such unless corroborated
* the prose sounds written, not generated
* transitions widen the frame instead of abruptly switching topics

## Editorial standard

The goal is not merely to imitate investigative outlets, but to write with the same discipline: ambitious narrative, factual clarity, and visible editorial judgment.
